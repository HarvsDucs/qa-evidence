GRADE 5 SCIENCE — QUARTER 4 SUMMATIVE QUIZ
Topic: The Water Cycle
Time: 30 minutes                                   Score: ____ / 15

Name: _______________________          Section: ____________

PART A — MULTIPLE CHOICE. Circle the letter of the best answer. (10 points)

1. What is the process by which liquid water changes into water vapor?
   A. Condensation   B. Evaporation   C. Precipitation   D. Collection

2. Which of the following provides the energy that drives the water cycle?
   A. The wind   B. The Moon   C. The Sun   D. Ocean currents

3. Clouds form when water vapor cools and changes back into tiny liquid droplets. This process is called:
   A. Condensation   B. Evaporation   C. Runoff   D. Transpiration

4. Rain, snow, sleet, and hail are all forms of:
   A. Evaporation   B. Condensation   C. Precipitation   D. Infiltration

5. The release of water vapor from the leaves of plants is called:
   A. Perspiration   B. Transpiration   C. Respiration   D. Precipitation

6. Water that flows over the land surface toward rivers and lakes is called:
   A. Groundwater   B. Runoff   C. Vapor   D. Dew

7. About what percent of Earth's surface is covered by water?
   A. 25%   B. 50%   C. 71%   D. 90%

8. Which of these is the correct order of the main stages of the water cycle?
   A. Precipitation → Evaporation → Condensation
   B. Evaporation → Condensation → Precipitation
   C. Condensation → Evaporation → Precipitation
   D. Evaporation → Precipitation → Condensation

9. When water soaks into the ground, the process is called:
   A. Infiltration   B. Evaporation   C. Condensation   D. Sublimation

10. Why are forests important to the water cycle?
    A. They stop the rain from falling
    B. Trees release water vapor and help rain soak into the ground
    C. They make the water salty
    D. They have no effect on the water cycle

PART B — TRUE OR FALSE. Write T or F. (5 points)

11. ____ The water cycle has a definite beginning and end.
12. ____ Most of Earth's fresh water is stored in ice and groundwater.
13. ____ Condensation happens when water vapor is heated.
14. ____ The same water on Earth has been recycled for millions of years.
15. ____ Dew on grass in the morning is an example of condensation.

============================================================
ANSWER KEY — FOR TEACHER USE ONLY. DO NOT DISTRIBUTE TO STUDENTS.
============================================================

PART A
1. B — Evaporation is the change from liquid to vapor.
2. C — The Sun's heat powers evaporation and drives the whole cycle.
3. A — Condensation forms clouds as vapor cools at altitude.
4. C — All are forms of precipitation.
5. B — Transpiration is water vapor released by plant leaves.
6. B — Runoff flows over the surface into rivers and lakes.
7. C — About 71% of Earth's surface is covered by water.
8. B — Evaporation, then condensation, then precipitation.
9. A — Infiltration is water soaking into soil to become groundwater.
10. B — Trees transpire and forests slow runoff so water infiltrates.

PART B
11. F — It is a continuous cycle with no beginning or end.
12. T — Ice caps, glaciers, and groundwater hold most fresh water.
13. F — Condensation happens when water vapor COOLS.
14. T — Earth's water is continuously recycled.
15. T — Dew forms when vapor condenses on cool surfaces overnight.

Scoring guide: Part A = 1 pt each. Part B = 1 pt each. 80% (12/15) is passing.
