THE WATER CYCLE — READING HANDOUT (GRADE 5)

Water moves through our world in a continuous loop called the water cycle.
The sun heats the surface of oceans and lakes, turning liquid water into
water vapor. This process is called evaporation.

As the vapor rises into cooler air, it condenses into tiny droplets that
form clouds. This process is called condensation.

When the droplets in a cloud grow heavy enough, they fall back to Earth as
rain, snow, or hail. This process is called precipitation. The fallen water
collects in rivers, lakes, and oceans, or soaks into the soil as groundwater,
and the cycle begins again.

Remember: the water you drink today may once have fallen as rain on a
dinosaur. Earth has been recycling the same water for billions of years.
