INTRODUCTION

Water on Earth is always moving. The water cycle describes how water moves
from the oceans to the sky, falls back to the land, and returns to the sea.
The sun provides the energy that drives the entire cycle.

Tandaan: ang "water cycle" ay tinatawag ding "hydrologic cycle" sa mas
mataas na baitang.

PART 1: EVAPORATION

When the sun heats the surface of oceans, lakes, and rivers, liquid water
changes into water vapor. This process is called evaporation. Plants also
release water vapor through their leaves in a process called transpiration.

Gawain 1: Sa iyong sagutang papel, magbigay ng dalawang halimbawa ng
evaporation na nakikita mo sa inyong bahay.

PART 2: CONDENSATION

As water vapor rises, the air becomes colder. The vapor cools and turns back
into tiny liquid droplets that gather around dust particles. Millions of these
droplets form clouds. This process is called condensation.

Tandaan: kapag umiinom ka ng malamig na tubig sa baso, ang tubig sa labas ng
baso ay halimbawa ng condensation — hindi ito tumagos mula sa loob!

PART 3: PRECIPITATION AND COLLECTION

When cloud droplets grow too heavy, they fall as rain, snow, or hail. This is
precipitation. Water then collects in rivers, lakes, and oceans, or soaks into
the ground — and the cycle begins again.

Gawain 2: Gumuhit ng diagram ng water cycle. Lagyan ng tamang label ang bawat
proseso: evaporation, condensation, precipitation, at collection.

Gawain 3: Sagutin sa isang pangungusap — bakit hindi nauubos ang tubig sa
mundo kahit araw-araw itong sumisingaw?

PAGTATAYA

Isulat ang TAMA kung wasto ang pangungusap at MALI kung hindi:
1. The sun is the main source of energy of the water cycle.
2. Condensation happens when water vapor turns into liquid droplets.
3. Precipitation only falls as rain.
